<p>A <b>marker pen</b>, <b>fineliner</b>, <b>marking pen</b>, <b>felt-tip marker</b>, <b>felt-tip pen</b>, <b>flow</b>, <b>marker</b> or <b>texta</b> (in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>) or <b>sketch pen</b> (in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>), is a <a href="/wiki/Pen" title="Pen">pen</a> which has its own <a href="/wiki/Ink" title="Ink">ink</a>-source, and a tip made of porous, pressed fibers such as <a href="/wiki/Felt" title="Felt">felt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SBTCModule6_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SBTCModule6-1">[1]</a></sup> A permanent marker consists of a container (glass, aluminum or plastic) and a core of an absorbent material. This filling serves as a carrier for the ink. The upper part of the marker contains the nib that was made in earlier time of a hard felt material, and a cap to prevent the marker from drying out. Until the early 1990s the most common solvents that were used for the ink were <a href="/wiki/Toluene" title="Toluene">toluene</a> and <a href="/wiki/Xylene" title="Xylene">xylene</a>. These two substances are both harmful and characterized by a very strong smell. Today, the ink is usually made on the basis of alcohols (e.g. <a href="/wiki/1-propanol" class="mw-redirect" title="1-propanol">1-propanol</a>, <a href="/wiki/1-butanol" class="mw-redirect" title="1-butanol">1-butanol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Diacetone_alcohol" title="Diacetone alcohol">diacetone alcohol</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cresols" class="mw-redirect" title="Cresols">cresols</a>).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lee Newman patented a felt-tipped marking pen in 1910.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In 1926 Benjamin Paskach patented a "fountain paintbrush"<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> as he called it which consisted of a sponge-tipped handle containing various paint colors. Markers of this sort began to be popularized with the sale of <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Rosenthal" title="Sidney Rosenthal">Sidney Rosenthal</a>'s Magic Marker (1953) which consisted of a glass tube of ink with a felt wick. By 1958 use of felt-tipped markers was commonplace for a variety of applications such as lettering, labeling, and creating posters.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> The year 1962 brought the development of the modern fiber-tipped pen (in contrast to the marker, which generally has a thicker point) by Yukio Horie of the Tokyo Stationery Company (which later became <a href="/wiki/Pentel" title="Pentel">Pentel</a>).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types">Types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Permanent_marker">Permanent marker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Permanent marker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Permanent_marker" title="Permanent marker">Permanent markers</a> are porous pens that can write on surfaces such as glass, plastic, wood, metal, and stone. The ink is generally resistant to rubbing and water, and can last for many years. Depending on the surface and the marker used, however, the marks can often be removed with either vigorous scrubbing or chemicals such as acetone.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Highlighters">Highlighters</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Highlighters">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Highlighter" title="Highlighter">Highlighters</a> are permanent markers used to highlight and cover over existing writing while still leaving the writing readable.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-permanent_marker">Non-permanent marker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Non-permanent marker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>A non-permanent marker (also called a <i>whiteboard marker</i> or <i>dry-erase marker</i>) uses an erasable ink, made to be used on a slick, non-porous writing surface, for temporary writing with <a href="/wiki/Overhead_projector" title="Overhead projector">overhead projectors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Whiteboard" title="Whiteboard">whiteboards</a>, and the like. They may also be used by children, making marks that are easy to clean. The erasable ink does not contain the toxic chemical compounds <a href="/wiki/Xylene" title="Xylene">xylene</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Toluene" title="Toluene">toluene</a> as have been used in permanent markers.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Wet_erase_marker" class="mw-redirect" title="Wet erase marker">Wet erase markers</a> are another version that are used on overhead projectors, signboards, whiteboards, and other non-porous surfaces.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Security_marker">Security marker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Security marker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Special "<a href="/wiki/Security" title="Security">security</a>" markers, with <a href="/wiki/Fluorescence" title="Fluorescence">fluorescent</a> but otherwise invisible inks, are used for marking valuables in case of <a href="/wiki/Burglary" title="Burglary">burglary</a>. The owner of a stolen, but recovered item can be determined by using <a href="/wiki/Ultraviolet_light" class="mw-redirect" title="Ultraviolet light">ultraviolet light</a> to make the writing visible.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Election_marker">Election marker</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Election marker">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Marker pens with <a href="/wiki/Election_ink" title="Election ink">election ink</a> (an indelible dye and often a <a href="/wiki/Photosensitive" class="mw-redirect" title="Photosensitive">photosensitive</a> agent such as <a href="/wiki/Silver_nitrate" title="Silver nitrate">silver nitrate</a>) used to mark the finger, and especially the <a href="/wiki/Cuticle" title="Cuticle">cuticle</a>, of voters in elections in order to prevent <a href="/wiki/Electoral_fraud" title="Electoral fraud">electoral fraud</a> such as double voting. The stain stays visible for a week or two and may also be used to assist in <a href="/wiki/Vaccination" title="Vaccination">vaccinations</a> in <a href="/wiki/Developing_country" title="Developing country">developing world</a> communities and <a href="/wiki/Refugee" title="Refugee">refugee</a> camps.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Porous_point_pen">Porous point pen</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Porous point pen">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <i>porous point pen</i> contains a point that is made of some porous material such as <a href="/wiki/Felt" title="Felt">felt</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ceramic" title="Ceramic">ceramic</a>. <a href="/wiki/Technical_drawing" title="Technical drawing">Draftsman</a>'s pens usually have a ceramic tip since this wears well and does not broaden when pressure is applied while writing.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Dialectal_variations">Dialectal variations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Dialectal variations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The use of the terms "marker" and "felt-tipped pen" varies significantly among different parts of the world. This is because most English dialects contain words for particular types of marker, often <a href="/wiki/Genericized_trademark" class="mw-redirect" title="Genericized trademark">generic</a> brand names, but there are no such terms in widespread international use.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Asia">Asia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Asia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, felt-tip pens are referred to as "sketch pens" because they are mainly used for sketching purposes whereas the permanent felt-tip markers are referred to as just "markers". In <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, marker pens are simply called markers. In the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>, a marker is commonly referred to as a "<a href="/wiki/Pentel" title="Pentel">Pentel</a> pen", regardless of brand. In <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, a marker pen is referred to as "Spidol". In <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, marker pens are referred to as "sign pens," "name pens," or "felt pens." In Japan, permanent pens are also referred to as "Magic" (from a famous pen brand name).</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, the term "marker" usually refers only to large-tip markers, and the terms "felt-tip" and "felt pen" usually refer only to fine-tip markers. Markers in Australia are often <a href="/wiki/Genericized_trademark" class="mw-redirect" title="Genericized trademark">generically</a> called "texta", after a brand name of a type of <a href="/wiki/Permanent_marker" title="Permanent marker">permanent marker</a>. Some variation in naming convention occurs between the states, for example in Queensland the brand name "nikko" has been commonly adopted.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="French-speaking_countries">French-speaking countries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: French-speaking countries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The French term for felt-tip marker is "<a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stylo-feutre" class="extiw" title="fr:Stylo-feutre">feutre</a>". For larger markers, the name "marqueur" is also used, and permanent markers are called "<a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marqueur_permanent" class="extiw" title="fr:Marqueur permanent">marqueur permanent</a>" or "marqueur indélébile".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The common German term for felt pen is <i>Filzstift</i> or "Filzschreiber" (colloquial "Filzer") or "Fasermaler". These are often used by children for sketching purposes, and should not be toxic. A highlighter is called <i>Textmarker</i> or "Leuchtstift" (the verb "leuchten" means to shine or to glow / the noun "Stift" means pen). Permanent markers are usually referred to as <i><a href="/wiki/Edding" title="Edding">Edding</a></i> after the leading brand for markers.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Spanish-speaking_countries">Spanish-speaking countries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Spanish-speaking countries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Spanish-speaking_countries" class="mw-redirect" title="Spanish-speaking countries">Spanish-speaking countries</a>, common terms for markers are <i>rotulador</i>, <i>marcador</i>, and <i>plumón</i>. Highlighting markers are known as <i>rotulador fluorescente</i>, <i>resaltador</i>, <i>destacador</i>, and <i>fosforito</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_Zealand">New Zealand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: New Zealand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The generic terms for fine-tipped markers are usually "felt pen" or "felts". Large permanent markers are called 'vivids' after a popular brand sold there.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Russia.2C_Lithuania.2C_Ukraine_and_the_Balkans">Russia, Lithuania, Ukraine and the Balkans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Russia, Lithuania, Ukraine and the Balkans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Smaller felt-pens (colorful ones used by children) are generally called <i><a href="/wiki/Flo-Master" title="Flo-Master">Фломастер (Flomaster)</a></i>, and permanent markers are called <i>Маркер (Marker)</i>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Romania">Romania</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Romania">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The generic term used for most felt-tip pens in <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> is "carioca" (pl. "carioci"), after the brand name of the first commercialised felt tips in Romania during the communist period. In recent times, the English word "marker" has been adopted (spelled as in English but with the plural "markere") and is used especially when referring to the permanent and highlighter variety of felt-tip pens.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="South_Africa">South Africa</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: South Africa">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The term "Koki" is used for both felt pens and markers, by South Africans, as well as the standard "marker".</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Canada_and_United_States">Canada and United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Canada and United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the United States, the word "marker" is used as well as "magic marker", the latter being a <a href="/wiki/Genericized_trademark" class="mw-redirect" title="Genericized trademark">genericized trademark</a>. The word "sharpie" is also now used as a genericized trademark.</p>
<p>In Canada and the US, "Magic Marker" is sometimes used to refer to "reveal markers" for "magic picture books" where the colors of a picture are revealed by a colorless marker. <a href="/wiki/Sharpie_(marker)" title="Sharpie (marker)">Sharpie</a> is a popular brand of permanent markers used for labeling. Markers are also sometimes referred to as felt-pens or felts in some parts of Canada.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Italy">Italy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Italy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a> the word "pennarello" is generally used.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Czech_and_Slovak_republic">Czech and Slovak republic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marker_pen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Czech and Slovak republic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a> and also <a href="/wiki/Slovak_language" title="Slovak language">Slovak</a> languages the general word used is "fixa".</p>
